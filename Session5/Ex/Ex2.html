Taylor Swift



Taylor Alison Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer and songwriter. She is someone who possesses a narrative style of composing music, the ability to constantly transform her art, and a profound cultural influence.

Swift is considered a representative figure in the popular music industry and is the focus of all public attention. Swift has released six studio albums, with the first four being sent to country radio, beginning with her debut album Taylor Swift (2006). She followed this with Fearless (2008), which explored pop and country influences, and the singles "Love Story" and "You Belong with Me" that helped her gain mainstream recognition. Swift followed that with her third studio album, Speak Now (2010), which featured a more rock-influenced style, and her fourth studio album Red (2012), which added electronic elements, and spawned her first number-one single on the Billboard Hot 100, "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together". In 2014, Swift shed her "country princess" image and released her fifth album 1989 (2014), which drew inspiration from 1980s synth-pop and featured the chart-topping hits "Shake It Off", "Blank Space" and "Bad Blood". The media scrutiny inspired her to release her sixth studio album Reputation (2017), which drew on hip hop influences and featured the number-one single "Look What You Made Me Do".
